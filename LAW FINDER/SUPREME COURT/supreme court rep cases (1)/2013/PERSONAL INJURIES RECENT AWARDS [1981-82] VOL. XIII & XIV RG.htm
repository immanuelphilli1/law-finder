PERSONAL INJURIES: RECENT AWARDS [1981-82] VOL. XIII & XIV RGL 167—171

 

(Unless otherwise stated all awards are for road accident cases.)

1. Fatal Injuries

ATTAH v. NYAMESON (Owusu-Addo J., Kumasi, 27 May 1981). Deceased male, master carpenter, aged 30, earning ¢500 a month. Survived by widow, four infant children and aged mother as dependants. Award: ¢77,000 (¢72,000 dependency for widow and children; ¢5,000 dependency for mother).

CLARKSON v. STATE TRANSPORT CORPORATION (Cecilia Koranteng-Addow J., Accra, 15 August 1980). Deceased male, farmer and blacksmith, aged 50, earning ¢3,000 per annum. Survived by widow and nine dependant children. Award: ¢11,000 (¢8,000 dependency; ¢3,000 funeral expenses).

DAPAAH v. BUSUMURU (Abban J., Accra, 6 February 1981). Deceased male, farmer and apprentice welder, aged 21, earning ¢1,460 per annum. Survived by widow aged 23, a 4-year old daughter and 59-year old mother as dependants. Widow likely to re-marry. Award: ¢11,500 (¢7,000 maintenance for daughter; ¢2,000 for widow; ¢1,000 for mother and ¢1,500 funeral expenses).

FLETCHER v. OPPONG (Twumasi J., Sekondi, 11 January 1982). Deceased male, photographer, aged 32, earning ¢1,000 a month. Died from wounds sustained when shot by police. Award: ¢250,934.50. (¢240,000 loss of earnings at 20 years' purchase; ¢9,834.50 funeral expenses and ¢1,100 medical expenses).

LARTEY v. ALLOTEY (Abban J., Accra, 13 March 1981). Deceased schoolgirl, aged 12. Award: ¢6,282 (¢5,500 general damages; ¢772 general and other expenses and ¢10 police report).

MARKO v. AMANKWA (Lamptey J., Kumasi, 17 February 1983). Deceased male, aged 22, employee of defendant, earning ¢130 per month. Award: ¢26,000 general damages.

ZEWU v. RAILWAY & PORTS AUTHORITY (Cecilia Koranteng-Addow J., Accra, 17 October 1980). Deceasad male, master tailor, aged 21, earning ¢30 daily. Survived by widow aged 21 (likely to re-marry), 60-year old mother, two sisters aged 25 and 18 years respectively and a brother. Award: ¢12,100 (¢3,000 funeral expenses; ¢500 mental distress for brother; ¢600 mental distress for mother; ¢8,000 reasonable expectation of pecuniary advantage inclusive of ¢2,000 for widow).

2. Multiple Injuries

BAAH v. GHANA COCOA MARKETING BOARD (Ansah-Twum J., Sekondi, 26 March 1981). Female, farmer, aged 60, earning ¢2,500 per annum. Sustained head injury and contusion of chest. Hospitalised for unspecified period; outpatient for four months. Resultant general weakness with post-traumatic headache, dizziness and chest pains; unable to walk unaided. Permanent incapacity assessed at 50 per cent. Award: ¢13,266.50 (¢11,666.50 loss of future income; ¢800 pain and suffering and ¢800 loss of amenities).

BROOKMAN-AMISSAH v. OWUSU BENTUM CONSTRUCTION LTD. (Osei-Hwere J., Cape Coast, 20 March 1981). Male medical practitioner, aged 39, earnings unspecified. Suffered cut under chin; broken bone in left index finger; contusion of left side of chest; compound fractures of left leg, i.e. fracture of mid-shaft of femur, communited fracture of knee, fracture of tibia and fibula and fracture of bone of big toe; contusion of kidney resulting in passing of blood in urine. Hospitalised for 14 months during which he underwent four major operations on left leg. Resultant inability to make complete fist with left hand; left leg fused at the knee and shortened by 1½ inches requiring special shoes; inability to stand to operate and examine patients properly; left leg swelling on sitting down for any length of time; inability to drive car and impaired enjoyment of sex. Award: ¢176,395 (¢40,000 car; ¢2,000 essential drugs; ¢6,395 wife's visits, i.e. transport, boarding and lodging; ¢6,000 advance rent for clinic; ¢72,000 hired driver at ¢3,600 a year for 20 years; ¢50,000 pain and suffering, loss of amenities and inconvenience for rest of life).

OMARI-SASU v. AFFUL (Amua-Sekyi J., Kumasi, 17 March 1983). Female, headteacher, aged 44, earning ¢845.16 per month. Burst liver with massive internal haemorrhage; severe contusion of the chest with massive haemothorax in the right chest; tear of the spleen and shock. Hospitalised for three months and a week; outpatient for unspecified period. Resultant palpitation, headaches and dizziness; pain in right flank and inability to exert herself physically during housework. Prematurely retired from Ghana Education Service as a result of severe injuries. Award: ¢98,220 (¢1,420 special damages; ¢20,000 pain and suffering; ¢76,800 loss of earnings at ten years' purchase).

KWESI v. AGBOSO (Wutoh J., Koforidua, 9 March 1981). Female farmer and trader, aged 39, allegedly earning ¢3,000 annually and ¢600 monthly from farming and trading respectively. Sustained laceration of scalp right parental region; fracture of neck and left femur and vaginal bleeding. Hospitalised for two weeks; out-patient for unspecified period. Resultant displacement of greater trochanter, hence unable to walk without stick; muscular atrophy of vaginal lining. Permanent incapacity assessed at 70 per cent. Award: ¢21,000 general damages.

LARIBU v. ATTORNEY-GENERAL (Abban J., Accra, 16 April 1981). Male, trotro driver, aged over 55 years, earning ¢30 a day. Injuries from stray-bullet fired by policeman; trachea shifted; large transverse wound at back extending from spine to scapular; wound antero laterally on left chest wall at seventh interspace; fractures and tenderness and crepitation along eighth, ninth and tenth ribs posteriously; large amount of air and blood in left pleural cavity; laceration and contusion of left lower lobe and left lung partly collapsed. Hospitalised for five weeks; outpatient for three months. Resultant pains in chest and shortness of breath on slight exertion; tender flat scars on sixth interspace to spine and mid axillary at sixth and seventh interspaces and also on left chest and medial aspect of left arm. Also diminished expansion and tenderness on compression of left hemithorax. Permanent incapacity assessed at 25 per cent and permanent deformity 3 per cent. Award: ¢16,367 (¢6,000 general damages; ¢10,000 (scaled down) loss of earnings; ¢367 special damages).

NKANSAH v. OBENG (Amuah J., Koforidua, 30 July 1981). Female, plywood seller, aged 45, earning ¢30-¢36 weekly. Laceration of forehead; impairment of hand limiting function; continuing pain in thumb; reduced vision; contused womb and vagina with resultant difficulty in intercourse and becoming pregnant. Award: ¢94,300 (¢1,000 personal loss; ¢16,000 cosmetic deformity and functional disability; ¢1,000 pain and suffering; ¢6,000 reduced vision; ¢300 loss of two teeth; ¢40,000 contused womb (excluding damages) for loss of consortium; ¢6,000 loss of earnings; ¢24,000 prospective loss at ten years' purchase).

ODI v. AKAKPO (Abban J., Accra, 5 March 1981). Female, trader and farmer, age unspecified, earning ¢800 a month. Swelling of ankle of nose; laceration of left hind side of back; contusion of right hand, severe pains in head and general body pains. Hospitalised for two weeks; out-patient for eight days and received herbal treatment for two months. Permanent disability assessed at 20 per cent. Permanent deformity assessed at 5 per cent. Award: ¢5,600 (¢2,500 pain and suffering; ¢1,000 medical and transport expenses and ¢2,100 loss of earnings).

QUAYNOR v. HORSU (Osei-Hwere J., Cape Coast, 9 September 1980). Boy, aged 7. Loss of two incissors, cut inside mouth; laceration on forehead and left heel. Hospitalised for two weeks. Resultant scar on left side of face. Award. ¢785 (¢500 pain and suffering; ¢285 extra nursing services).

3. Arm

TEYE v. BOANYO (Hayfron J., Sunyani, 3 March 1981). Male, fitter, aged 22, earning ¢20 per day. Compound fracture of left forearm. Hospitalised for seven weeks. Resultant amputation of left arm above elbow. Award: ¢138,460 (¢20,800 loss of earnings; ¢555 medical expenses; ¢105 food for seven days; ¢15,000 mental distress; ¢102,000 (scaled down) loss of prospective earnings at 33 years' purchase).

4. Wrist

OMARI-SASU v. AFFUL (Amua-Sekyi J., Kumasi, 17 March 1983). Male, circuit court judge, age and earnings unspecified. Injuries mainly to left wrist which was put in P.O.P. Period of hospital treatment unspecified. Award: ¢20,970 (¢12,630 special damages; ¢4,000 pain and suffering; ¢4,200 loss of use of vehicle at ¢50 per day for 84 days and ¢140 transport to visit wife at hospital at ¢20 per day for 14 days).

5. Spine

DAGARTI v. STATE GOLD MINING CORPORATION, TARKWA (Twumasi J., Sekondi, 30 September 1981). Male, shovel headman, aged 38, earning ¢7.12 a day at time of accident but wage increased to ¢17.28 a day in 1980. Sustained fracture dislocation on twelfth dorsal and first lumbar vertebrae as a result of cave-in at the mine. Hospitalised on 18 August 1978 and still on admission at date of judgment. Resultant injury to spinal cord and paralysis of both legs; atrophy of leg muscles and thus will never walk again except in wheel chair. Permanent disability assessed at 100 per cent. Award: ¢201,003.44 (¢15,000 pain and suffering; ¢65,000 permanent disability; ¢2,221.44 special damages; ¢103,782 (scaled down) loss of prospective earnings; ¢15,000 loss of amenities).

6. Leg

ANNAN v. BUABIN (Okunor J., Cape Coast, 15 January 1982). Girl, aged 12. Compound fracture of tibia and fibula of left leg; contusion of left thigh and pain in left leg. Hospitalised for three months; outpatient for unspecified period. Resultant deformed swollen left leg and walks with a limp; scar on median side of lower leg; falls down when doing exercises and after walking short distances and thus has had to stop school. Award: ¢7,500 (¢4,500 pain and suffering; ¢2,000 permanent deformity and ¢1,000 loss of amenities of life).

BADU v. ABAYIE (Twumasi J., Sekondi, 27 April 1983). Male, aged 50, purchasing clerk, grade I. Sustained bilateral compound fracture of tibia and fibula. Hospitalised for four months and underwent two operations. Resultant osteosynthesis of left leg; insufficient callus formation on union of fractured bones, limps on left leg and uses clutches in walking. Permanent incapacity assessed at 30 per cent. Award: ¢75,000 (¢10,000 pain and suffering; ¢4,500 loss of amenities; ¢26,000 permanent disfigurement and incapacity; ¢28,000 loss of prospective earnings and ¢6,500 special damages).

QUANSAH v. AKRONG (Abban J., Accra, 2 April 1981). Male, work unspecified, age 34, earning ¢130 a month. Sustained compound communited fractures of right and left tibia and fibula with segments of tibia showing through wound. Hospitalised for nine and half months; out-patient for six months. Resultant mal-united fractures; extensive scaring of right leg; walks with a limp; eversion and inversion of right ankle joint reduced by about a half. Continuing pain in waist and right leg and instability in right leg. Permanent incapacity including cosmetic disfigurement assessed at 52 per cent. Award: ¢39,608.85 (¢12,000 pain and suffering; ¢4,000 loss of amenities; ¢22,000 (scaled down) loss of future earnings for 21 years; ¢1,500 transport expenses; ¢108.85 special damages).

VIDEGLA v. QUAINOO (Wuaku J., Cape Coast, 31 March 1981). Female, beans and gari seller, aged between 45-50, earning ¢5,000 annually. Knocked down unconscious while walking on pavement. Injuries mostly to legs. Hospitalised for two months during which both legs from waist down put in P.O.P. Continued as outpatient but period unspecified. Resultant post-traumatic arthrosis in right leg; leg swollen and ankle movement limited 66 per cent; muscular tenderness on waist; movement of lumbal spine painful and limited 66 per cent; multiple scars on right leg and ankle; permanent headache and sleeplessness. Permanent incapacity assessed at 45 per cent. Award: ¢31,349 (¢16,333 loss of earnings; ¢5,000 pain and suffering; ¢10,000 cosmetic deformity and permanent disability; ¢16 special damages).

YANKEY v. KANYAN (Twumasi J., Sekondi, 15 June 1981). Female, kenkey seller and coconut oil manufacturer, aged 38, earning ¢450 per month. Sustained compound fracture at dislatend of fermoral bone; knee joint capsule torn apart together with its lendons and ligaments on the antero and lateral aspect of knee; dislocation of left petalla. Hospitalised for over seven months and underwent treatment for one year and four months thereafter. Resultant probably completed formity and shortness of leg; ankylosis and impaired movement of left knee. Incapacity assessed at 60 per cent. Award: ¢93.150 (¢8,000 pain and suffering; ¢27,000 loss of prospective earnings; ¢10,350 loss of earnings during treatment; ¢35,000 functional disability and deformity of leg; ¢10,000 loss of amenities; and ¢2,800 special damages).
