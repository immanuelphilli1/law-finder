2. Multiple Injuries

ADU v HISSON (Williams J, Koforidua, 7 February 1989). Male, middle school pupil, aged 14. Contusion of face and lacerated wound before right ear and below right nostril, fracture dislocation of right femur shaft in proximal 1/3, fracture dislocation of left tibia in mid-shaft, distortion of left ankle and located wound ventral of ankle, contusion of pelvis, permanent impairment at 50 per cent, cosmetic disfigurement at 5 per cent. Global award: (¢75,000) excluding ¢9,000 costs).

AKPANAVOR v BADU (Dove J, Accra, 11 April 1989). Female aged 50, farmer, earning ¢150,000 per annum. Partially frozen right shoulder joint, cosmetic deformity of frontal scalp, pains in neck, arm and leg. Cannot walk without shoes or slippers, no longer able to dance agbadza. Admitted to hospital for three weeks, out-patient for one month. Permanent incapacity of 40 per cent. Award: ¢653,667 (¢433,667 loss of income; ¢100,000  pain and suffering; ¢20,000 loss of amenities; ¢100,000 costs).

ASAMOAH v ARMAH (Williams J, Koforidua, 6 February 1990). Female, age and income unspecified; treated and discharged same day, out-patient for about a month. Award: ¢136,750 (¢115,000 general damages; ¢21,750 special damages excluding ¢20,000 costs).

AZURE v ALI (Farkye J, Bolgatanga, 18 August 1989). First plaintiff: male, employee of Public Works Department, age and earnings unspecified. Hospitalised for two weeks, unconscious for eight days, sustained contusion with multiple laceration on head and trunk, vision impaired in left eye, conspicuous scars on the face and scalp, disfigurement 70 per cent, needs a stick to help him walk. Award: ¢50,000.

BELLA v AMANKRAH (Sarpong J, Cape Coast, 13 June 1990). Male, aged 43, professional driver, income unspecified, married with two wives and five children, eldest seventeen, youngest three years. Hospitalised for 80 days, out-patient for sometime, lost right arm, can no longer drive. 80 per cent permanent incapacity. Award: ¢2,000,000 excluding ¢50,000 costs.

BOADI v GYENI (Piesare Ag J, Accra, 4 November 1988). Male, farmer aged 39, footballer and tennis player, income unspecified. Lost consciousness at time of accident, fractured right thigh and bruises on body, underwent surgery on thigh, nail inserted in bone, in bed for three months, re-admitted for second surgical operation for removal of nail in bone, has to use shoe calipers, physiotherapy for nineteen months, permanent incapacity assessed at 50 per cent. Award: ¢461,734.22 (¢116,734.22 special damages; ¢150,000 general damages; ¢75,000 pain and suffering; ¢100,000 loss of amenities; ¢20,000 costs).

BREMANG v OWIREDU (Piesare Ag J, Accra, 5 May 1989). Male, age unspecified, farmer earning about ¢10,000 per annum, unconscious after accident, hospitalised for sometime, three fractured ribs, crushed fractures of second lumbar vertebrae, fractured left collar-bone, fracture of both bones of right forearm, fracture dislocation of sixth cervical vertebrae, bedridden, lost manhood as a result of injuries sustained, incapacity assessed at 75 per cent, unable to maintain farm and to pay loan of ¢15,000 taken from the bank for development of farm. Award: ¢770,000 (¢15,000 bank loan; ¢90,000 loss of earnings for nine years; ¢250,000 injuries including loss of manhood; ¢150,000 pain and suffering; ¢250,000 loss of amenities excluding ¢30,000 costs).

DICKSON v TIMBERS (Adjabeng J, Sekondi, 30 October 1987). Male, age unspecified, just completed apprenticeship and working for master, income assessed at ¢1,000 per month, compound fracture of left tibia and fibula, laceration and contusion of right arm, contusion of left side of face, head injury. Award: ¢77,000 (¢40,000 pain and suffering; ¢25,000 loss of amenities; ¢12,000 loss of earnings excluding' ¢12,000 costs).

DUKU v OWUSUA (Lartey J, Kumasi, 3 July 1990). Female child aged 10. Hospitalised for two and a half months, unconscious for some time; deep laceration in the occipital region of the skull; physically deformed in the spine and pelvic region; right leg now shorter than the other; limps when walking; fracture of the lower third of the left and right femur bones, resultant pain in waist when walking; 50 per cent functional disability, 25 per cent cosmetic disability. Award: ¢950,000 (¢850,000 general damages; ¢100,000 special damages excluding ¢50,000 costs).

DZIWORNU v ASAKUM ENGINEERING LTD (Essilfie-Bondzie J, Sekondi, 26 July 1990). First plaintiff: Male, age unspecified, welder and taxi driver earning ¢300 a day, hospitalised for three days. Award: ¢60,000 (¢30,000 pain and suffering; ¢30,000 loss of earnings).

hird plaintiff: Female child, age unspecified, lost consciousness at the time of accident, hospitalised for six weeks, received herbal treatment, for some time, fracture of the upper third of right humerus, abrasion of right thigh and calf, suffered from epistaxis. Award: ¢200,000 for pain and suffering excluding ¢20,000 costs.

KABUNGA v AMOH (Lartey J, Kumasi, 3 September 1990). Male, aged 35, professional driver; income unspecified. Hospitalised for two months sixteen days, received herbal treatment for some time; severe crush of the tibia and fibula, went through surgery; left leg amputated above the knee, stump poorly healed; further operative fashioning required to fit artificial limb; 70 per cent disability. Award: ¢1,410,000 (¢200,000 pain and suffering; ¢1,050,000 loss of prospective earnings; ¢40,000 loss of amenities of life; ¢60,000 special damages; ¢60,000 for nourishment and special diet excluding ¢50,000 costs).

KUMTSIA v KUM (Essilfie-Bondzie J, Sekondi, 22 June 1989). Male, farmer aged 50 years, income unspecified, acid thrown at his face, burns on left face, neck, eyelids and nose, suffers chronic sinusitis, chronic shoulder pain, scars on left shoulder, neck and back, operation on eye and nose. Award: ¢530,000 (¢250,000 disability; ¢100,000 disfigurement; ¢50,000 loss of amenities of life; ¢50,000 pain and suffering; ¢100,000 special damages excluding ¢30,000 costs).

MAHAMA v MUSTAPHA (Benin J, Tamale, 2 June 1989). Male, farmer aged 52, married with two wives and ten children, earning ¢8,000 per annum. Fracture of left collar-bone, fracture of ribs and contusion of chest and left lung. Award: ¢176,114 (¢14,000 loss of earnings; ¢150,000 pain and suffering; ¢2,114 special damages; ¢10,000 costs).

MARFO v ODURO (Adjabeng J, Sekondi, 11 December 1987). Male, age, occupation and income unspecified, laceration over right eye which was sutured, laceration at right hip also sutured, multiple abrasions over both knees, painful and tender right shoulder indicating contusion at joint, temporary incapacity at 15 per cent, disfigurement at 10 per cent. Award: ¢35,020 (¢11,300 bag of gari destroyed; ¢17,520 sum of money lost; ¢1,200 transportation to hospital; ¢5,000 pain and suffering excluding ¢4,000 costs).

 

MENSAH v JOHN BITAR & CO LTD (Essilfie-Bondzie J, Sekondi, 31 July 1990). Male, age, income and occupation unspecified, multiple lacerations on left arm, neck, face, knees and elbows, lost left middle finger. Award: ¢500,000 (¢400,000 for disfigurement of left middle finger; ¢100,000 pain and suffering excluding ¢20,000 costs).

MILLS v MINISTRY OF HEALTH (Lutterodt J, Accra, 14 February 1990). Male, farmer, age unspecified, earning ¢75,000 a year. Out-patient for five weeks, herbal treatment for eleven months, can no longer wear shoes, play football or attend film shows and concerts. Award: ¢1,219,100 (¢150,000 pain and suffering; ¢200,000 loss of amenities; ¢750,000 loss of prospective earnings; ¢100,000 for the injury itself excluding ¢50,000 costs).

NCHONAH v APPIAH, MENSAH v GENERAL DEVELOPMENT CO LTD (GDC LTD) HAMMOND v GDC LTD; CONSOLIDATED) (Adjabeng J, Sekondi, 26 May 1989).

First plaintiff: Male, commercial farmer. Owned 78 acres farm, age and income unspecified, hospitalised for two months, suffered deep cut on left leg, four toes on left foot broken, already had a bad right leg, limps on both legs, can no longer walk around farm on supervision. Award: ¢680,200 (¢30,200 hospital expenses; ¢200,000 pain and suffering; ¢50,000 loss of amenities of life; ¢400,000 loss of earnings excluding ¢40,000 costs).

OSUMANU v GONJA (Benin J, Tamale, 2 June 1989). Female, trader aged 50 years, earning ¢1,200 a month, unconscious for first two months after accident, fractures of tibia and fibula of both legs, hospitalised for six months, permanent deformities - left leg fracture now pseudoathrosis thirteen centimetres below knee cap causing depression of one centimetre, injuries to right car and face. Award: ¢414,000 (¢300,000 loss of earnings; ¢4,000 medical expenses; ¢100,000 prospective earnings; ¢10,000 costs).

POKU-APPIAH v BONSU (Lartey J, Kumasi, 28 July 1989). Female, trader, income and age unspecified, fracture of left ninth and tenth ribs, contusion of pelvis, laceration of face and left forearm, abrasion on left eyebrow, contusion of right side of head, out-patient for four months, invalidity 15 per cent, disfigurement 5 per cent. Award: ¢544,000 (¢200,000 pain and suffering; ¢20,000 transport expenses; ¢35,000 herbal treatment; ¢40,000 extra food and nourishment; ¢47,000 hospital expenses and damages; ¢180,000 loss of earnings for one year; ¢2,500 medical and police reports; ¢20,000 costs).

QUAYE v LARTEY (Lutterodt J, Accra, 16 January 1989). Second plaintiff: Male, aged 22 years, part-time driver earning ¢3,500 a month; total loss of function in left eye; pain in right eye; attacks of dizziness when lifting heavy loads; can no longer drive or work as a labourer. Award: ¢1,979,210. (¢100,000 pain and suffering; ¢100,000 loss of amenities of life; ¢1,260,000 loss of future earnings; medical expenses ¢519,210 excluding ¢25,000 costs).

SULEMANA v OWUSU-ANSAH (Lartey J, Kumasi, 29 June 1989). Male, age, income and vocation unspecified, hospitalised for four days, received herbal treatment for two months, suffered compound fracture of the lower third of the left tibia and fibula with dislocation, abrasion and contusion of left forearm, abrasion of left shoulder region, contusion and haematoma of left upper arm and multiple abrasions of face and laceration of left half of the upper lip.  Award: ¢111,800 (¢73,000 pain and suffering; ¢10,000; disfigurement; ¢16,800 hospital expenses; ¢12,000 costs).

TAMAKLOE v ANDREWS AGENCIES (Lutterodt J, Accra, 27 May 1988). Male, aged about 45, driver mechanic in the Ghana Army, income unspecified. Hospitalised for some time, out-patient for 28 months, pain in walking, difficulty in using arm, elbow, wrists, fingers and neck, could not stand to watch football, wife had left him. Award: ¢458,000 (¢200,000 pain and suffering; ¢150,000 loss of amenities of life; ¢108,000 loss of future earnings).

ZAKARI v ALHASSAN (Benin J, Tamale, 6 June 1989). Male, aged 49, farmer, trader and tom-tom beater, earning about ¢13,600 per annum, torn medial collateral ligament, hospitalised after accident, amputation of distal part of right foot through metatarsal bones, received further treatment in hospital, pains to parts of body affected by accident persisting for quite some time, difficulty in walking on amputated foot. Award: ¢618,300 (¢18,300 medical expenses; ¢400,000 pain and suffering; ¢180,000 loss of earnings based on 15 years' purchase scaled down including ¢20,000 costs).

3. Leg

NUJOR v WOTORSI (Ampiah JA, Accra, 25 May 1987). Female, age, occupation and income unspecified, engaged in business activities outside regular work, lower limb shortened by 2 cm, walks with a limp, still engaged in regular employment and receiving normal salary. Award: ¢170,000 (¢60,000 loss of earnings from business activities outside regular work; ¢50,000 transport; ¢60,000 pain and suffering, incapacity and deformity including future treatment, excluding ¢25,000 costs).

4. Teeth

AZURE v ALI (Farkye J, Bolgatanga, 18 August 1989). Second plaintiff: Female, age and vocation unspecified, lost four upper teeth, 40 per cent deformity. Award: ¢30,000.

5. Miscellaneous Awards

ADOFO v ADOFO (Piesare J, Accra, 12 January 1989). Wife-petitioner, Assistant Director of Education, had four children aged 15, 12, 10 and 8 years all in custody of petitioner, two attending secondary school, two attending preparatory school. Award: ¢320,000 (¢20,000 a month maintenance allowance; ¢300,000 arrears of maintenance).

ABIREH v ATTORNEY-GENERAL (Benin J, Tamale, 11 July 1988). Income earning cargo truck, earnings assessed at ¢4,000 daily. Award: ¢241,589.74 (¢111,589.74 cost of repairs; ¢120,000 loss of earnings including ¢10,000 costs).

ABUBAKARI v WESTERN DAGOMBA DISTRICT COUNCIL (Benin J, Tamale, 16 May 1989). Income earning bus, estimated daily earnings in 1976, ¢60. Award: ¢88,225.71 (¢3,600 loss of use; ¢1,125 cost of repairs and interest at bank rate ¢3,500.71; general damages ¢50,000; costs ¢30,000).

BEMPAH v AYITEVI (Asare Kwapong J, Ho, 25 January 1989). Damage to Datsun saloon car, used by wife in second-hand clothing trade at various markets, car chartered at ¢5,000 per day in the absence of damaged car. Award ¢517,865.50 (¢117,815.50 cost of repairs; ¢50 police report; ¢400,000 general damages and loss of use; ¢10,000 costs).

CHONAH v APPIAH, MENSAH v GDC LTD (CONSOLIDATED) (Adjabeng J, Sekondi, 26 May 1989). Toyota bus, income earning vehicle, loss of use for 300 days; daily returns unspecified or proved. Award: ¢900,000 on arbitrary basis.

NSIAH v ASARE (Williams J, Koforidua, 30 July 1987). Income earning vehicle, earning ¢30,000 weekly. Award: ¢271,000 (¢150,000 loss of earnings for a period of five weeks; ¢111,020 cost of repairs; ¢10,000 general damages excluding ¢7,000 costs).

QUAYE v LARTEY (Lutterodt J, Accra, 16 January 1989). Loss of VW Passat car used for taking children to school and carting goods for restaurant. Pre-accident value ¢60,000, spending ¢800 a day for doing rounds, ¢22,400 a month scaled down to 20,000 a month for four months. Award: ¢80,000.
