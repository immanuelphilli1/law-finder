MISCELLANY-AT-LAW: TRIBUTE TO THE LATE MRS JANET DANIELS, FIRST EDITOR OF THE REVIEW OF GHANA LAW [1989-90] VOL. XVII RGL 1—4

KOM ENOCH D.

 

*

IT is with deep regret and sorrow that the Review records—to her evergreen memory—the sad passing away on 20 January 1991 of Mrs Janet Daniels, Barrister-at-law and first Editor of the Review of Ghana Law. Glowing tributes were paid to her at the Memorial and Thanksgiving Service held for her on 25 January 1991 at the Ridge Church, Accra. The Review proudly reproduces below the tribute paid to her honour by her successor in Office.

Tribute by Mr S Y Bimpong-Buta, Editor of the Ghana Law Reports and Review of Ghana Law

We are assembled here this morning to mourn the passing away of Mrs Janet Daniels, a distinguished member of the Ghana Bar. I have to say that I have not had the privilege of seeing and meeting with her since the past two years for reasons beyond my control. And only last week, I said to myself: the first thing I would do is to call on Janet at her residence with a view to paying my respects and to continue to tap from her rich experience as an expert in legal publications. Little did I know that the last thing I would be called upon to do for her is to appear at her funeral service and deliver a tribute to her honour and memory.

Mrs Janet Daniels retired as the Acting Editor of the Ghana Law Reports and Chief Executive of the Council for Law Reporting on 8 April 1980 after sixteen years' continuous service. She had in fact been associated with the writing, preparation, editing and publication of the Ghana Law Reports and the Current Cases some eight years before the establishment of the Council for Law Reporting in 1972. She had in 1965 been appointed by the General Legal Council as the Assistant Editor of the Ghana Law Reports. And when in July 1968 the General Legal Council decided to publish the Review of Ghana Law as the premier Law Journal in Ghana, the Council, unhesitatingly appointed Mrs Janet Daniels in 1969 as the first Editor. For more than ten years she served with distinction as the Editor of the Review of Ghana Law and at the same time, served as the Assistant Editor of the Ghana Law Reports.

There can be no doubt that Mrs Janet Daniels by her work, did make a significant and invaluable contribution to the administration of law and justice in Ghana. Writing on the assessment of a Decade of Publication of the Ghana Law Review under the editorship of Janet Daniels, the Hon Mr Justice Apaloo, the former Chief Justice said:

"The Review has enabled practitioners and academic lawyers alike to examine the policy justification of some of the (judicial) decisions and subject them, if need be, to critical analysis... It can hardly be gainsaid that the Review has made a significant contribution to the development of Ghanaian law especially the application of known legal concepts not only to the facts but to the particular circumstances of Ghanaian jurisprudence."

Given the transience of human life—there is time for coming and a time for going—Mrs Janet Daniels, as I have already said, retired from the services of the Council almost ten years ago and soon after her retirement, I wrote and published in the 1980 Review of Ghana Law a tribute in her honour—a tribute which in my opinion is worth repeating on the occasion of her permanent retirement from this life. I said:

"Mrs Janet Daniels, the untiring editor of the Review of Ghana Law during the first decade of its life, willingly gave up the editorship of the Review as from 6 April 1979 to enable her get more time to perform her duties as Ag Editor of the Ghana Law Reports. That was a timely warning to the Review that it might, sooner or later, permanently lose its devoted mother who had nurtured it from its birth to its present enviable national and international reputation as the official law journal of Ghana.

It is therefore with the deepest regret that the present Editor of the Review records the formal retirement, on medical grounds, as from 8 April 1980, of Janet Daniels after her long distinguished and devoted services to the Ghana Law Reports and the Review of Ghana Law.

The Review would like to place on record with admiration and a deep sense of gratefulness, the immense and invaluable contribution made by Mrs Janet Daniels to the preparation and publication of the Ghana Law Reports and the Review of Ghana Law. Perhaps, her greater contribution is to be found in her work as the Editor of the Review. She nurtured the Review from its birth to its present position. She always insisted that articles and Notes and Comments submitted for publication in the Review should be sharp, crisp and as far as possible accurate. She not only edited (with a heavy hand) articles received for publication but she herself found time to write invaluable articles for publication in the Review, ie articles such as Statute Law in Ghana, Trial by Jury, Patterns and Fashions in Reported Judgments and her contribution to an interesting article: Symposium on Defamation of Women. With such a contribution lasting over a period of sixteen years, we all must share the sad regret that the Council for Law Reporting would be the poorer with the retirement of Mrs Janet Daniels. The Review and its readers would like to wish Mrs Janet Daniels a very happy retirement."

Her retirement from active work and this life regrettably ended on Sunday, 20 January 1991. The best tribute which we, left behind in the Council for Law Reporting, can pay to her memory is to ensure that the excellent professionalism, capacity for sustained hard work, sense of urgency to work on hand and very high editorial standards which characterised her work would, at the very least, be maintained if not bettered.

I would like on behalf of the Chairman and Board Members of the Council for Law Reporting, the general staff and on my own behalf to extend to Professor Ekow Daniels, formerly Dean of the Faculty of Law, University of Ghana, the widower, the children and the bereaved family our sincere and deepest condolences. May she rest in everlasting peace.

Thank you.
