PERSONAL INJURIES [1989-90] VOL. XVII  RGL 253—260

RECENT AWARDS

 

 

(Unless otherwise stated all awards are for road accident cases)

1. Fatal Injuries

APPIAH v BAFFOUR (Aryee Ag J, Accra, 26 July 1989). Deceased male, aged 38 years, driver earning about ¢50,000 per month, survived by a widow and four children aged 8, 6, 4 and 2 years respectively, a dependent father and mother aged 80 and 55 years respectively. Award: ¢4,680,000 (¢4,000,000 loss of dependency; ¢500,000 loss of expectation of life; ¢180,000 funeral expenses excluding costs of ¢30,000).

ASARE v ASANTE (Aryee Ag J, Accra, 5 April 1989). Deceased male, aged 50, teacher, earning ¢4,000 a month. Married with eight children, ages ranging between 26 and two years. Award: ¢1,106,000 (¢576,000 loss of dependency; ¢480,000 loss of expectation of life; ¢50,000 costs).

DAGBUIE v ASAFO (Amuah J, Ho, 24 February 1989). Deceased female, aged 60, farmer, income unspecified, spending ¢5,000 a month on her surviving three children aged 15, 20 and 22 years, suffered pain for a day. Award: ¢500,000 (¢300,000 general damages; ¢150,000 special damages and ¢50,000 pain and suffering).

DRAMANI v SOLI (Aryee Ag J, Accra, 16 December 1988). Deceased female, aged 40, charcoal seller and merchant, earning ¢480,000 per annum, survived by husband and six other dependants aged 19, 17, 15, 12, 7 and 5 respectively, hospitalised for a few days. Award ¢4,240,000 (¢3,600,000 loss of dependency in respect of six surviving children based on 15 years' purchase, ¢100,000 funeral expenses; ¢40,000 drugs; ¢500,000 loss of expectation of life excluding ¢50,000 costs).

IDRISA v SAOUD BROTHERS AND SONS (Adjabeng J, Sekondi, 23 December 1987). Deceased male, aged about 59, trader in timber products, survived by a widow and other dependants including three minors aged 19,14 and 10 respectively. Award: ¢133,000 (¢108,000 loss of dependency in respect of widow and three minor children based on 6 years' purchase; ¢20,000 loss of expectation of life; ¢5,000 funeral expenses excluding ¢7,000 costs).

KOOMSON v EDU (Adjabeng J, Sekondi, 20 January 1989). Deceased school boy, aged 17 years, earning ¢400 a day for making coalpots. Award: ¢250,000 for loss of expectation of life.

Second plaintiff: Deceased infant aged four and a half years, attending school. Award: ¢100,000 for loss of expectation of life.

NARH v TEYE (Ammah J, Accra, 14 April 1989). Deceased female, aged 32, trader in textiles, earning ¢12,000 a month, died three to four hours after accident, survived by three infant children aged 13, 8 and 4 years and a 67-year old dependant mother. Award: ¢1,055,000 (¢602,667 loss of dependency; ¢30,000 loss of expectation of life; ¢115,000 funeral expenses, ¢6,000 pain and suffering; ¢301,333 for commissioner of income tax excluding ¢30,000 costs).
