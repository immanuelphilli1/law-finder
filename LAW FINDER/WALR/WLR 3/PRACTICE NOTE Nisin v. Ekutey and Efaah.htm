                                                        April 23, 1957

                                              Nisin v. Ekutey and Efaah  

                                                       PRACTICE NOTE

                                                      High Court, Central Judicial Division, Divisional Court

                                                        Adumua-Bossman J

 
 

Practice-Jurisdiction of High Court-Application for leave to serve writ out of jurisdictioT1r-Civil Procedu1'C Rules, 1954, Ord. 2, r. 4, and Ord. 11, r. 1.

On an application by motion ex parte under the Civil Procedure Rules, Ord. 2, T. 4, for leave to issue a writ outside the jurisdiction ADUMUA­BOSSMAN J., following Societe Generale de Paris v. Dreyfus, held that although a court or judge will not go into the merits of a plaintiff's claim as disclosed, yet it will satisfy itself that the plaintiff has a probable cause of action and, if no cause of action be disclosed, then leave will not be granted.

Case referred to:

Societe Generale de Paris v. Dreyfus (1885) 37 Ch.D. 225 (C.A.).

S. G. D.
