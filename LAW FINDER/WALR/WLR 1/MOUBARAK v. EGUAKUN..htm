                                                                                 MOUBARAK v. EGUAKUN.

                                                                                                                   

pg 88

(Supreme Court of the Gold Coast, Western Judicial Division,   (Windsor-Aubrey J.), January 31, 1956.)

Landlord and Tenant-Recovery of possession by landlord--Rent in arrears -Effect of Rent Control Ordinance, c. 2 of 1952, s. 11 (1) (a).

The defendant was a tenant, on a monthly tenancy at a rent of £20, of premises in Sekondi belonging to the plaintiff. In September and October, 1955, he failed to pay the rent due from him and the plaintiff sued in the Sekondi District Magistrate's Court for the recovery of possession of the premises under section 11 (1) (a) of the Rent Control Ordinance. It was established at the hearing that the defendant had paid the rent for September direct into the plaintiff's bank account on November 14, which was the day before the summons to appear in the magistrate's court was served upon him, and that the rent for October had been paid on December 7, which was the day the summons was returnable. The defendant had previously defaulted with the payment of rent and in respect of this the plaintiff, in June, 1955, had obtained judgment for £60, which sum he had recovered in full from the defendant.

Held, the purpose of the Rent Control Ordinance is to increase rather than to diminish the security already given by the common law to tenants. The provisions of section 11 (1) are mandatory only in the sense that an order for the recovery of possession of premises affected by the Ordinance may not be granted unless the prescribed conditions are satisfied: the section should not be interpreted as requiring the issue of an order whenever the conditions are satisfied.

As to Determination of a Term, Relief from Forfeiture for Non­payment of Rent, see Halsbury, Hailsham ed., Vol 20, pp. 264--267, paras. 297-299.

For the Rent Control Ordinance, c. 2 of 1952, see Laws of the Gold Coast, Supplement 1952-1954, Vol 1, p. 225.

APPEAL from the decision of the District Magistrate, Sekondi, on January 4, 1956, granting an order for the recovery of possession to! the plaintiff landlord.

WINDSOR-AUBREY J. In this case the defendant appeals against the judgment ?f the Districts Magistrate at Sekondi, granting the~ plaintiff possession of certain premises on the grounds of forfeiture £01 non-payment of rent under section 11 (1) (a) of the Rent Control Ordinance. The object of this legislation is to afford greater, not less' protection to the tenant than existed under the old common lawl Even under the common law, possession was rarely given for non! payment of rent if the arrears were paid into court .. The learned I magistrate took the "view that the defendant was a bad payer as this  pg 89 as his second default, he having also defaulted in June, 1955. He does not, however, seem to have taken account of the fact that the defendant has been a tenant for eight years and that these two defaults are the only ones that have occurred. To meet the first default the defendant had to borrow money and no doubt had to pay heavily for the privilege. The second default occurred towards the end of the same year and doubtless arose because of the first default, and the financial embarrassment occasioned thereby. There is no history of continual default by the defendant, and as the magistrate did not refer to section 11 (3) of the said Ordinance, which provides for the suspension of an Order and its discharge if arrears are paid within a reasonable time, I do not think he can have had it in mind.

In my opinion the circumstances of this case did not justify an order for possession. The appeal is therefore allowed and the order for possession set aside. As to costs the defendant is allowed the costs of this appeal, but as he was in arrears with his rent at the time the summons was issued he must pay the costs in the magistrate's court.

Appeal allowed.

S. G. D.
