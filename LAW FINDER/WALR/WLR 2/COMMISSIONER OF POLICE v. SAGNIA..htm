                                             COMMISSIONER OF POLICE v. SAGNIA.

                                                              Supreme Court of the Colony of the Gambia

                                                                                         Miles C.J.

 

   pg 209

Criminal Law-Appeals-Error 'in charge sheet-Power oj Supreme Court to alter finding of trial court-Criminal Procedure Code, c. 23, s. 290 (1) (a) (ii).

The appellant was convicted of breaking and entering a dwelling­house and stealing. Breaking and entering a dwelling-house is an offence by the Criminal Code, c. 21, s. 281 (1). The charge was drawn with clear reference to that offence but the charge sheet, in error, spoke of it as arising under section 283.

Held, the statement and particulars of offence made plainly clear to the appellant the nature of the offence with which he was charged and for which he was rightly convicted. The erroneous reference to section 283 of the Criminal Code therefore caused no failure of justice, and provided a proper occasion for the exercise by the court of its power under the Criminal Procedure Code, c. 23, s. 290 (1) (a) (ii), to alter the finding of the trial court. The conviction should therefore be altered to one of housebreaking and stealing contrary to sections 281 (1) and 252 of the Criminal Code.

APPEAL from a conviction for breaking and entering a dwelling-house given in the Bathurst Magistrate's Court on October 6, 1956.

Bridges, Assistant Attorney-General, for the Commissioner of Police. The appellant appeared in person.

MILES C.J. This appellant was convicted in the Bathurst Magis­trate's Court of breaking into and entering the dwelling-house of .one Fatou J allow and stealing a number of articles of jewellery and money to the value of £6 16s. 0d. He was sentenced to twelve months' imprisonment with hard labour. He now appeals against conviction and sentence.

This was a pure question of fact. The Magistrate accepted the evidence of the prosecution witnesses and I can see no reason why he should not have done so. The appellant told the police a number of inconsistent stories and he did the same in court. In these circumstances it is not surprising that the learned Magistrate disbelieved him.

Mr. Bridges has very properly drawn my attention to the fact that the charge was laid under section 283 of the Criminal Code. This was plainly an error for section 281 (1). Both the statement and the particulars of offence made it perfectly clear to the appellant that he was charged with breaking and entering a dwelling-house and I agree with Mr. Bridge's contention that this defect in the charge could not  pg 210 possibly have occasioned a failure of justice. In pursuance of my powers, under section 290 (1) (a) (ii) of the Criminal Procedure Code, I alter the conviction to one of housebreaking and stealing contrary to sections 281 (1) and 252 of the Criminal Code.

The appellant has previous convictions for stealing and the sentence of twelve months' imprisonment cannot be said to be in any way excessive. The appeal is consequently dismissed.

Appeal dismissed.

S. G. D.
