        Motor Traffic-Order of disqualification from driving-Whether order per .. , admissible in case tried under Trial of Petty Criminal Cases Ordinance Trial of Petty Criminal Cases Ordinance, c. 25, s. 5.

       Motor Traffic-Failure to produce licence-Whether an offence connected wit driving a motor  vehicle-Motor Traffic Ordinance, c. 155, ss. 70 an, 86 (1).

The appellant was charged and convicted under the Motor Traffic: Ordinance, s. 70, of failing to produce his driving licence and was disqualified from driving for twelve months. The magistrate at the triJ used the procedure permissible under the Trial of Petty Criminal Ca Ordinance, c. 25, and on appeal against sentence the point was taken that the powers of the magistrate were strictly circumscribed by section 5 J that Ordinance and did not include the power to order disqualification from driving.

Held, although the Trial of Petty Criminal Cases Ordinance, c.

s. 5, does not, in an action tried under the Ordinance, expressly lirni the punitive powers of a court to those punishments dealt with in th: section, the terms of the proviso to the section, in limiting the order that can be made in addition to the said punishments, imply that t intention of the section is so to limit the punitive powers of the court For this reason and, on the application of the principle that a penal statute must be interpreted strictly, the cases which a court is empower' to try under the provisions of the Trial of Petty Criminal Case, Ordinance are limited to those for which the appropriate punishment would not exceed those prescribed by section 5 thereof. Thus, a case I which the punishment, required by law or considered by the court be appropriate in the circumstances, lies outside those permitted section 5, are not triable under the Trial of Petty Criminal Cases Ordinance.

Semble, the offence of failing to produce a licence is not an offence , " connected with the driving of a motor vehicle" within the meaning of the Motor Traffic Ordinance, c. 155, s. 86 (1), and as the punishment of disqualification may only be imposed for such offences, an order for disqualification was ultra vires the court in the present case.

APPEAL against sentence on a conviction on August 21, 1956, in the Rombo St. Mary Magistrate's Court on a charge of failing to produce a driving licence, under the Motor Traffic Ordinance, c. 155, s. 70.

Bridges, Acting Attorney-General, for the Chief Superintendent of Police.

Forster for the appellant.

MILES J. The appellant was convicted in the Rombo St. Mary Magistrate's Court of failing to produce his driving licence, contrary to section 70 of the Motor Traffic Ordinance, c. 155. He was fined £3 and disqualified from holding a driving licence for twelve months. He now appeals against the order imposing disqualification.

The only ground upon which the order for disqualification is challenged in the petition of appeal is that it was an excessive punish­ment in the circumstances, but the learned Acting Attorney-General, who appeared for the Crown on this appeal has raised the question whether the order was legal.

The case was tried under the provisions of the Trial of Petty Criminal Cases Ordinance, c. 25. Section 3 of this Ordinance is in the following terms:-

" Notwithstanding anything contained in the Criminal Proce­dure Code or the Protectorate Courts Ordinance, or any other law in force in the Gambia relating to the trial of criminal cases, 3 magistrate having jurisdiction to try any offence, may, if so requested by the person conducting the prosecution or if the magistrate himself deems such procedure expedient, try such offence in the manner hereafter provided by section 4 of this Ordinance. "

Section 5 provides that:­" No sentence of

(a) imprisonment exceeding one month; or

(b) fine exceeding £5, with or without imprisonment in default of payment thereof, such imprisonment not to exceed one month; or pg102 (c) corporal punishment exceeding twelve strokes with an approved instrument, shall be passed in the case of a conviction for any offence tried under the provisions of this Ordinance, and no such sentence shall include more than one of the foregoing punishments.

" Provided that a court may, in addition to any of the fore­going punishments, order the restitution, confiscation or forfeiture of any property which is or forms part of the subject matter of any charge, where such restitution, confiscation or forfeiture is provided for by law."

The question is whether section 5 empowers the court to impose an order for disqualification. It is clear from section 86 of the Motor, Traffic Ordinance, c. 155, that disqualification is imposed by an; " order." It cannot be said that such an order comes within the: proviso to section 5 of the Trial of Petty Criminal Cases Ordinance, c. 25, and it seems to me that the proper interpretation of this section is that the only punishment which can be imposed by the court when case is tried under the procedure of this Ordinance is one of th punishments permitted by paragraphs (a), (b), (c) of section 5 together, with one of the orders specified in the proviso. In other words th maxim: "Expression unius est exclusion alterius "applies. If it had been intended by the legislature that orders of a punitive nature shall not in general be excluded by section 5 the proviso would not hay been necessary. The meaning of this section, however, is by no mean clear and this is a case where it would be appropriate to apply th observations, based upon a series of cases, which are to be found in Maxwell on the Interpretation of Statutes, 7th ed., p. 244 :

Where an equivocal word, or ambiguous sentence leaves reasonable doubt of its meaning, which the canons of interpretation fail to solve, the benefit of the doubt should be given to the' subject and against the Legislatl1l'e which has failed to explain itself. "

Here it may be said that the whole section is equivocal.

This is an ordinance for the trial of " Petty" Criminal Cases. The

'word" Petty" appears nowhere in the body of the Ordinance but i is clear that the cases which the court is empowered to try under th provisions of this Ordinance are limited to those for which the appropriate punishment would not exceed that prescribed by section 5, Such a case would not include one in which the court was either obliged by law, or considered it appropriate to impose, an order for, disqualification.

I would add that it is doubtful whether this particular offence could be said to be an offence " connected with the driving of a motor; vehicle" within the meaning of section 86 (1) of the Motor Traffic Ordinance, c. 155. This point, however, was not fully argued before pg103 me and I express no opinion upon it. I hold that the order of dis­qualification imposed in this case was illegal. The appeal is allowed and the order of disqualification is quashed.

Appeal allowed and order quashed.

S. G. D.
