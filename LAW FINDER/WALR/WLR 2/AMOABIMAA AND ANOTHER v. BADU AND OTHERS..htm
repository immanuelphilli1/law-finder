 January 28, 1957

                                 AMOABIMAA AND ANOTHER v. BADU AND OTHERS.

                                                      West African Court of Appeal, Gold Coast Session

                                                                   Coussey P., Korsah C.J., Verity Ag.JA

 

 

              Practice-Appeals to Privy Council-Power of West Af1'ican Court of Appeal to order stay of execution-West African (Appeal to Privy Council) Order "in Council, 1949, s. 8.

The plaintiffs were successful in the West African Court of Appeal on April 3, 1956. On July 27, 1956, the defendants obtained the leave of the court to appeal to the Privy Council and on July 31 filed. an application for a stay of execution pending the determination of the appeal. An order was made by the court but the plaintiffs then moved for this to be set aside on the ground that it was a nullity for want of jurisdiction.

Held, section 8 of the West African (Appeal to Privy Council) Order in Council, 1949, by which the court may give directions as to execution or otherwise of a judgment appealed from, gives the court power to suspend execution or to order that judgment be carried into execution " when granting leave to appeal." In view of this express provision the court has no power to give directions on an application made at a lapse of time after it has granted leave to appeal.

APPLICATION by motion on notice by the plaintiffs for setting aside an order of the West African Court of Appeal given on October 30, 1956, ordering execution of a judgment of the court on April 3, 1956, against security, for the performance of the order to be made by the Privy Council, on the grounds of nullity for want of jurisdiction.

Hayfron-Benjamin, Snr., for the opposers. pg 215

 The ruling of the court was given by COUSSEY P.

On July 27, 1956, the defendants obtained final leave to appeal to Her Majesty-in-Council from the judgment of this court dated April 3, 1956. On July 31, 1956, the defendants filed an application by motion for an order to stay execution of the judgment appealed from pending the hearing and determination of the appeal to Her Majesty­in-Council. The application coming on for hearing on October 30, 1956, this court, purporting to act under article 8 of the West African (Appeal to Privy Council) Order in Council, 1949, ordered possession of the land in dispute to be delivered to the plaintiffs in terms of the judgment on the condition that the plaintiffs should give security for the due performance of such order as Her Majesty's Judicial Committee of the Privy Council should make in the appeal.

On November 28, 1956, however, the court differently constituted in the suit of Osei Bonsu III v. Kwasi Adu (1), considered its power to order stay of execution after, and not when granting, leave to appeal, and made the following ruling:

" On this application the occasion is afforded to the court to consider whether it has power and jurisdiction, after a lapse of one month from the date of granting leave to appeal to the Privy Council, to order that the execution of the judgment be suspended pending the appeal.

" The relevant words of the article read ' the court shall have power when granting leave to appeal, either to direct that the said judgment shall be carried into execution etc.'

" We consider that these words regulate and limit the power of this court to order stay of execution to the time when leave to appeal is granted and not thereafter. If it had been intended that this court could, at any time after granting leave to appeal, direct that the judgment appealed from be carried into execution or that execution be suspended, the Order in Council would have so provided in terms other than the express direction that the court shall exercise such power when granting leave to appeal.

" In our opinion article 7 cannot be read with article 8 of the Order so as to empower this court to order stay of execution at any time in respect of an appeal pending before the Privy Council. " We rule therefore that this application cannot now be enter­tained by this court and it is accordingly refused."

The respondents now apply to this court by motion on notice for an order to set aside our said order of October 30, 1956, on the ground that it is a nullity for want of jurisdiction in the court.

As we have already held, as set out in the ruling above referred to, that this court may give directions as to execution or otherwise of the pg216 judgment appealed from only at the time that final leave to appeal is granted, it follows that the order made herein on October 30, 1956, upon an application filed four days after the grant of leave to appeal was irregularly made. A judgment or order obtained by some step not warranted by the rules or capable of being sanctioned is wholly void and may be set aside: Smurthwaite v. Hannay (2). It seems to us that the order complained of is a nullity and that the respondents who are affected by it are entitled ex debito justitiae to have it set aside.

It is accordingly ordered that the order of this court of October 00, 1958, be and it is hereby set aside.

Each party will pay its own costs on this motion.

Application allowed.

S. G.'D.
