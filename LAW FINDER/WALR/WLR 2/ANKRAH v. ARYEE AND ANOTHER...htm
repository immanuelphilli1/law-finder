ANKRAH v. ARYEE AND ANOTHER..

         Supreme Court of the Gold Coast, Eastern Judicial Division, Divi­sional Court, Accra

                                                                              Windsor-Aubrey J.

 

 

Sale of goods - Credit-sale agreement with proviso for seizure of goods on default-Nature of 'vendor's remedy.

Sale of Goods-Guarantee-Construction of words "Guarantor' Antwi" on credit-sale agreement-Statute of Frauds, 1677, s. 4.

The first defendant entered into an agreement with the plaintiff for the purchase of a vehicle. The defendant took possession immediately but the purchase price was payable by instalments and a clause in the agreement provided for seizure of the vehicle on default of payment. The agreement contained the word "guarantor" followed by the signature of the second defendant. The first defendant defaulted and the plaintiff sued him for the balance of the outstanding instalments, joining the second defendant as a guarantor. The first defendant claimed that, by the agreement, the only remedy available to the plaintiff was seizure of the vehicle: the second defendant claimed there was insufficient memorandum in writing under the Statute of Frauds to bind him as a guarantor.

Held, (i) by the terms of the agreement the proviso for seizure in the event of default was an alternative remedy, at the option of the vendor, to an action for the recovery of the unpaid balance of the purchase price.

Delor v. Foli (1952) 14 W.A.C.A. 54 referred to.

[EDITORIAL NOTE. Delor v. Foli would appear to overrule Osei Kofi v. Mensah (1930) 1 W.A.C.A. 76.]

(ii) Although the word "guarantor" was used, this, without more, was too vague an expression to comprise a promise, by the second defen­dant, to answer for the debt, default or miscarriage of the first defen­dant. The guarantee-if indeed there was a guarantee-was therefore unsupported by sufficient note or memorandum in writing and could not be enforced against the second defendant. pg 252

Case referred to:

(1) Delor v. Foli (1952) 14 W.A.C.A. 54.

ACTION for the recovery of £530, being the balance due from the first defendant under a credit-sale agreement. The second defendant was sued as guarantor of the debt.

Francois for the plaintiff. Amoo-Lamptey for the first defendant. Aryee for the second defendant.

WINDSOR-AUBREY J. There is no dispute as to the facts and this case turns on construction of law. The plaintiff claims against the defendant £530 being the balance of money due and owing on the purchase of a lorry, registered No. AD 5827, and as against the second defendant the like sum as guarantor. His claim is founded on a written agreement.

'1'he first defendant denies liability, asserting that the plaintiff's only right is to seize the lorry under clause 4 of the agreement. The second defendant denies liability on the grounds that there is no sufficient memorandum in writing to bind him. All that he has done is to sign as follows: "Guarantor Antwi."

In my opinion the agreement is not difficult to interpret. Under clausAs 1, 2 and 3 the defendant agreed to buy the lorry for a certain sum payable by instalments. That created a debt. In my opinion clause 4 confers on the plaintiff an additional, alternative remedy of seizing the lorry if he so desires. He has an option to SUA for the debt or seize the lorry. It has been held in the case of Delor v. Foli (1) that provision for seizing a chattel is not inconsistent with a sale.

As to the second defendant I regard the word " guarantor '.! as a description of the signer just as the words" purchaser" and" witness" are in the same category. If the vendor chooses to be so vague it is his own fault. What is the guarantor supposed to be guaranteeing­instalments in arrear or that the defendant will not part with possession or sell the vehicle? In the circumstances of this case the word " guarantor" is far too vague to impose any liability.

In the result the plaintiff must succeed against the first defendant and fail against the second defendant.

There will be judgment for the plaintiff against the first defendant for the amount claimed, namely £53'0 and costs. The plaintiff's claim against the second defendant is dismissed with costs to the second defendant.

Judgment accordingly.

S. G. D.
