September 9, 1956

CHIEF SUPERINTENDENT OF POLICE v. SIBI.

Colony of the Gambia

Miles J.

 

     

       Motor Traffic- Vehicle parked without lights-Order of disqualification from driving-Whether an offence connected with the driving of a vehicle­Motor Traffic Ordinance, c. 155, s. 86 (I)-Motor Traffic Regulations, 1955, Regulation 32 (1).

Motor Traffic-Order of disqualification from driving-Whether order per­missible in case tried under Trial of Petty Criminal Cases Ordinance­Trial of Petty Criminal Cases Ordinance, c. 25, s. 5.

The appellant was charged and convicted under the Motor Traffic Regulations, 1955, Regulation 32 (I), of parking a vehicle without lights and was fined and disqualified from driving for six months. The magis­trate at the trial used the procedure permissible under the Trial of Petty Criminal Cases Ordinance, c. 25.

Held, (i) the offence of parking a vehicle without a light is not an offence "connected with the driving of a motor vehicle" within the meaning of the Motor Traffic Ordinance, c. 155, s. 86 (I), and as the punishment of disqualification may only be imposed for such offences the order in this case was ultra vires the court and could not stand.

(ii) The Trial of Petty Criminal Cases Ordinance, c. 25, s. 5, limits the range of punishments permissible for offences tried under the provi­sions of the Ordinance. An order of disqualification is not a punishment permitted by the Ordinance and therefore the order in this case, even if it were one permissible under the Motor Traffic Ordinance for the offence with which the appellant was charged, could not stand.

APPEAL against sentence on a conviction on August 21, 1956, in the Rombo St. Mary Magistrate's Court on a charge of parking a vehicle without a light, under the Motor Traffic Regulations, 1955, Regulation 32 (1).

Bridges, Acting Attorney-General, for the Chief Superintendent of Police.

Forster for the appellant.

MILES J. This appellant was convicted in the Rombo St. Mary Magistrate's Court of the offence of parking a motor-vehicle without a light contrary to Regulation 32 (1) of the Motor Traffic Regulations, 1955. He was fined £5, and disqualified from holding a driving licence for six months. He now appeals against sentence. The case was tried by the Magistrate under the provisions of the Trial of Petty Criminal Cases Ordinance, c. 25. It has already been held in this court in the case of Chief Superintendent of Police v. Sankareh (1) that an order for disqualification cannot be made in the case of a trial under this Ordinance and the appeal, so far as the disqualification is concerned, must succeed on that ground alone.

There is another ground, however, upon which the disqualification cannot be supported in this case. By section 86 (1) of the Motor Traffic Ordinance, c. 155, the power to disqualify is limited to:

•• any offence committed in connection with the driving of a motor­vehicle or any offence involving dishonesty towards a passenger or with respect to any goods or freight carried by the vehicle driven by that person."

It was held in R. v. Yorkshire (West Riding) Justices (2), which was decided under the corresponding section of the Motor Car Act, 1903, that allowing a motor-car to stand on a highway so as to cause an unnecessary obstruction thereof was not an offence " In connection with the driving of a motor car." Lord Alverstone there said at p.441:

.• the words 'Any offence in connection with the driving of a motor car,' when read with their context, in section 4 of the Motor Car Act, 1903', point to offences connected with the handling or manipulation of the car in the process of driving it."

That principle applies to this case and the order of disqualification was not one which could legally have been made by the court even if the pg100 case had not been tried under the provisions of the Trial of Pett~ Criminal Cases Ordinance, c. 25. The appeal is therefore allowed so far as the order of disqualification is concerned and the order is qua she as previously announced.

The appellant has eight previous convictions for traffic offences an the leaving of unlighted vehicles on the road at night is extremely~ dangerous and may well lead to serious accidents. In these circum stances it is impossible to say that the fine of £5 was in any way. excessive.

Appeal allowed in part; order quashed.

S. G. D.
