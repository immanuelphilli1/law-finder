JUDGMENT

RULING ABODAKPI, J:-

The application which triggered the preliminary objection, which is the issue before this court is entitled. MOTION ON NOTICE FOR EXTENSION OF TIME WITHIN WHICH TO APPLY FOR LEAVE TO APPEAL The motion paper showed that, applicant is praying for an order for extension of time to apply for leave to appeal. The reason assigned in support is that the appeal stands a good chance of success.AND EXHIBIT 'AD1', attached, showed that diligent efforts were made to secure a copy of the RULING given on 6' August 2009, and that applicant was not negligent. At the hearing counsel for the Republic/Respondent argued that appellant/applicant's right of appeal had not accrued. He referred to section 90 of the Armed Forces Act, Regulation 4 of the Armed Forces [Court Martial Appeal Court] Regulations and Regulations 4(1) of the Rules of Court (Court Martial Appeal Court). Counsel submitted that the combined effect of the rules is that an applicant/appellant has (90) ninety days as the prescribed period to apply to the officer commanding his unit/establishment. Regulation 4(1) of the Rules of court (Court Martial Appeal's Court) provides: "The period within which a person convicted by a court, other than a person sentenced to death must, as a condition precedent to the exercise of his right of appeal, present under regulation 4(a) of these rules to the appropriate authority a petition praying that his conviction be quashed shall be 90 days next following that on which the finding of the Court- Martial was promulgated". And section 90(a) of the Armed Forces Regulations provides: "Every person who has been tried and found guilty by a court Martial shall have a right of appeal to the Court Martial Appeal Court, in such form, manner and within..... "...such time as may be prescribed in terms of either or both of the following a. the legality of any or all findings. The two legislations reproduced above showed that the law giver contemplates a PERSON CONVICTED AND, EVERY PERSON WHO HAS BEEN TRIED AND FOUND GUILTY by a Court Martial". Even though applicant has been convicted after a full trial his application for LEAVE to appeal relates to the interlocutory order/finding that, he should enter his defence, because a prima fade case has been made against him. Under section 173 of the Criminal Procedure Code, Act 30/60 (as amended) a prima facie case per se cannot lead to a conviction. The meaning of, PRIMA FACIE would provide a proper context in which to look at the issues raised by this application and the preliminary objection. Prima facie evidence means: "that which, not being inconsistent with the falsity of the hypothesis... "...nevertheless raises such degree of probability in its favour that it must prevail if believed by the jury unless rebutted or the contrary proved". Another definition of what constitutes prima fade case is as seen in the case: SHER SINGH VRS JITENDRAHATH SEN [1931] 1 LR calc. 275 there Ghose J stated: "It (prima facie) means that there is ground for proceeding but prima fade case is not the same thing as 'PROOF' which comes later when the court has to find whether the accused is guilty or not guilty; 'PROOF' is nothing but belief according to the conditions laid down in the Evidence Act". Even though applicant has been convicted after a full trial he chose to attack the order/ruling of prima facie finding made against him, that he has a case to answer. There are a number of schools of thought on this type of application that has been made. The import of the application is that his submission of no case to answer was wrongly over-ruled. Hence the need for leave to appeal against it. Counsel for applicant and the respondent have not cited the exact rule of procedure in support of their arguments. The analogous reasoning adopted by counsel for respondent, wherein, he argued that the procedure for conviction after a full trial is applicable, is wrong. The submission made in that regard showed the difficulty he faced is stating clearly what the applicable rule of procedure really is. It is noted even with the criminal procedure Code, Act 30/60, there is a great difficulty in stating the law when a judge/court has wrongfully over-ruled a submission of no case to answer.I agree with the views expressed by MR E.A. OSEW in his article in the REVIEW OF GHANA LAW, on the topic, NO CASE TO ANSWER, at page 90 and 100. Whilst analyzing the case R.V. OSABUE MAASEYO [1957] 3 WALR 180. C.A, he stated the court of appeal made a distinction between the situation where there is some evidence which could go to the jury, and the situation where there is no evidence at that stage to go to the jury.He further stated that in the former position if there is subsequent conviction, the appellate court is entitled to look at all the evidence in the case, including evidence subsequently admitted after the wrongful rejection of the submission of no case. And in the other case [ie the latter] where there is no evidence that could have gone to the jury at the time the submission was made, the wrongful rejection of the submission amount to a wrongful decision on a point of law.That being so, the evidence adduced subsequently should not be accepted as part of "all the evidence in the case". And the appellate court must therefore be effectively precluded from considering it. As seen from the title of the motion as quoted above, counsel for applicant is seeking an extension of time as well as LEAVE. Under the Courts Act, Act 459/93 as amended and the regulations of this court there is NO RIGHT of appeal from an interlocutory rulings/orders and findings. It stands to reason that leave must be sought, but no useful purpose would be served in granting either the leave or extension of time for the applicant to appeal against the interlocutory order.I am of the view that the appropriate thing to do is to file a petition as provided by the regulations of this court, and appeal against the conviction entered against him after the full trial. In that application or petition of appeal, it would be appropriate to state as a ground of appeal, the position that applicant has taken, namely that his submission of no case to answer was wrongfully over-ruled. BY COURT: The preliminary objection is upheld, the application for extension for time within which to apply for leave to appeal is dismissed. COUNSEL MR DAVID VANDYIK FOR ANDREW DANIELS FOR APPLICANT. MAJOR FELIX KORBIEH FOR CAPT. BINDITI CHITOR FOR RESPONDENT.
