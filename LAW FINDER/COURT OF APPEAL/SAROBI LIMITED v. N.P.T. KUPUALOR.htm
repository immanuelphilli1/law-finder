JUDGMENT

OWUSU, J.A.

This is an appeal against the ruling of His Lordship Justice S.G. Baddoo, Justice of appeal (as he then was), sitting as an additional Justice of the Fast Track High Court, delivered on 6th September, 2001.

The Plaintiff/Respondent herein is a member and then chairman of the Association of Building and Civil Contractors of Ghana in the Central Region, while the Defendant was the National president of the said Association.

The plaintiff issued a writ of summons against the Defendant claiming the following reliefs:

(a) "A declaration that the Defendant has over stayed his term of office as the National President of the Association of Building and Civil Contractors and an order for his removal.”

(b) Appointment of an interim Management Committee."

(c) Appointment External Auditors to audit the accounts of the Association from January 15th 2000, to date."

(d) An order to restrain the Defendant from Exercising any power or performing any function as the National President of the said Association including without limitation the management and Control of the finances or accounts of the Association."

(e) An election of a National President to be held within a specified period under the auspices of National Electoral Commission."

The writ was accompanied by a statement of claim.

On 7/8/2001, the Defendant entered appearance but did not file a defence. On 8/9/2001, the plaintiff filed an application for summary Judgment under Order 14 rule 1 of the High Court Civil procedure Rules L.N. 140A as amended by L.I. 1127.

In the supporting affidavit the plaintiff averred that under the constitution of the Association, the National president and the cabinet appointed by him, constitute the steering Committee and they could hold office for a period of four (4) years from the date of the election of the National president.

Thus the Defendant having been elected National President on or about 15/1/96 his term of office expired on or about 14/1/2000.

On 16/8/2001, the Defendant filed a statement of Defence and an affidavit in opposition to the application for summary judgment.

On 22/8/2001, the Defendant filed a motion on notice to set aside the writ of summons and dismiss the suit under order 12 rule 24 of the Civil Procedure (High Court) Rules, L.N. 140A.

The Defendant averred in the affidavit in support of the motion that by failing to go to arbitration in contravention of Article 37 of the Constitution of the association, the plaintiff's action before the court is premature frivolous and vexations.

The application was opposed by Counsel for the plaintiff who contended that the Defendant's application was misconceived as the Defendant entered unconditional appearance instead of conditional appearance or appearance under protest.

Having entered unconditional appearance the Defendant could not proceed under Order 12 rule 24 to have the plaintiff's writ of summons set aside.

Under Order 12 (4) rule 24 which reads as follows:—

"A defendant before appearing shall be at liberty without obtaining an order to enter or entering a conditional appearance, to take out a summons or serve notice of motion to set aside the service upon him of the writ or of notice of the writ or to discharge the order authorising such service."

The learned trial judge after hearing both counsel refused to set aside the writ of summons and accordingly dismissed the application.

Dissatisfied with the ruling, the Defendant on 14/9/2001 filed Notice of Appeal to this court on the following grounds:

(a) "The learned trial judge erred in his conclusion that the National Executive is incompetent to deal with the dispute the subject matter of the suit.

(b) The learned trial judge erred in finding that the Defendant is in breach of Article 16 of the Constitution of the association of Building and Civil Contractors of Ghana (ABCCG).

After the dismissal of the application to set aside the writ the Court proceeded to hear the application for Summary Judgment filed by the plaintiff under Order 14 ru1e 1.

This application was opposed by counsel for the defendant who had by then filed a defence to the plaintiff's claim.

The crux of the affidavits in support of the application for summary judgment is that the Defendant has no defence at all to the cause of action.

Having heard both counsel and on the strength of their affidavits both for and against the grant of summary judgment, His Lordship decided in favour of the application and thus entered judgment in favour of the plaintiff for all the reliefs sought on 19/9/2001.

Dissatisfied with the ruling, the Defendant again filed another Notice of Appeal on 21/9/2001 with fresh grounds of Appeal. This Appeal is embodied in the same record of proceedings and the two Appeals given the same number, NO. CA 38/2001.

On 21/12/2001, counsel for the appellant without leave of the court, filed four additional grounds of appeal two of which touched on the first ruling refusing to set aside the writ of summons and the other two on the ruling on the application for summary judgment.

The statement of case filed covered the two Appeals in contravention of rule 20 (1) of the Court of appeal rules of 1997, C.I. 19 which requires that the Appellant files a written submission of his case based on the grounds of appeal as set out in the Notice of Appeal.

On 2/6/2003, the case was adjourned to 24/7/2003 for Judgment. On the adjourned date, counsel for the appellant's attention was drawn to the anomaly and was asked by the court to rectify it. The case was again adjourned to 20/11/2003 because of the legal vacation for Judgment.

Since then, the Court had to adjourn the case on two occasions because there was no proof of service of hearing notice to counsel for the Appellant who together with his client have not been attending court.

The case was adjourned for the last time to to-day and there has not been any reaction from counsel for the Appellant.

From all indications, counsel for the Appellant is not desirous of rectifying the anomaly and the appeal as it stands now is nebulous and same is accordingly dismissed.

R.C. OWUSU
