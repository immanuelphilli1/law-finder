JUDGMENT

OMARI-SASU J.A.

The present appeal was filed by the Original defendant herein Mr. Paul Ayitey Tetteh and it is against the ruling of AKAMBA J.A. sitting as an additional High Court Judge.

Only one ground of appeal was filed and it states as follows:—

"The trial Judge misdirected himself as to the actual prayer before him".

This ground of appeal, with respect to the solicitor for the appellant, lacks precision but in simpler language it may be recast as

"the trial judge erred in the decision

"he finally came to”.

The problem with this case in the court below was about an application of one B.A. Quarcoo to be substituted for Paul Ayitey Tetteh the original defendant as the substituted defendant.

The learned trial Judge granted the application for substitution on August 22, 2002. Thereafter then original defendant applied for the order for substitution to be set aside. This was refused and the present appeal emanates from the said refusal.

It is my considered view that to understand the present appeal and deal effectively with the ground of appeal three processes of the court below should meticulously be examined bearing in mind ORDER 52 RULE 3 AND 6 of L.N. 140A - THE HIGH COURT CIVIL PROCEDURE RULES, 1954 - which rules are applicable to our case.

The said three court processes to be examined are:

(a) The formal Application for Substitution.

(b) What transpired in the court below on August 22, 2002, the date the application was heard and granted and

(c) The Order for Substitution which was drawn up.

The operation part of Order 52 Rule 3 states;

(inter alia):—

"No motion shall be made without previous notice to the parties

"affected thereby. But the court or a Judge, if satisfied

"that the delay caused by proceeding in the ordinary way would

or might entail irreparable or serious mischief, may make any order

ORDER 52 RULE 6 also states (inter alia)

" If on the hearing of a motion or other application the court or

" a Judge shall be of the opinion that any person to whom notice has

" not been given ought to have or had such notice the court or

" a Judge may either dismiss the motion or application or adjourn

" the hearing thereof in order that such notice may be given”.

The formal application for substitution is at page one of the record and states:-

SUIT NO. 2986/91

" The occupants of Ga, Gbase and Korle Stools of Accra          Plaintiffs

                                           vrs:

" Paul Ayitey Tetteh (Substituted by B.A. Quarcoo Current

" Head of Onamroko-Adain family).                                        Defendant

" (a)   APPLICATION FOR SUBSTITUTION

" Application by Johnny Hansen & Co., Counsel for and on

" behalf of Mr. B.A. Quarcoo, Head of Family & lawful re-

" presentative of the ONAMROKO-ADAIN FAMILY praying this court

" for an order substituting Mr. B.A. Quarcoo currently elected

" head of family and lawful representative of the Onamroko-Adain

" family for Mr. PAUL AYITEY TETTEH and for such other order

" as this court may seem fit.

" Court to be moved on MONDAY the 5th day of August, 2002.

"                                                              (Sgd.) Johnny Hansen.

" The Registrar,

" High Court,

"Accra.

"                 AND TO the above-named Plaintiff-Respondent

"                  or their solicitor – D.A. Nii Amponsah Esq., Ga Mashie

“                 Chambers, No. 16 Gamashie Street, Korle Wokon, Accra”

Comments:- The formal application for substitution which was filed on July 16, 2002 was not meant to be served on the appellant as it completely leaves the appellant’s name out of those to be served. It does not also state whether it was an application on notice or an ex-parte application. Further since on 16/7/2002 Mr. B.A. Quarcoo was only applying to be substituted and the application had then not been heard it was wrong for him to be described as "substituted by B.A. Quarcoo etc".

This motion paper should have been made on notice to the plaintiffs and also to Paul Ayitey Tetteh, the Defendant, since he was alive and not dead. The motion which was moved in the Court below being Ex-Parte, offended against Order 52 Rule 3 of L.N. 140A of 1954.

(b) What transfirmed in the court below on August 22 2002 can be seen at page 13 of the record

      The operative part is as follows:—

" Applicant’s representative-Mensah Larkai is present

" Defendant is absent.

" Mr. Johnny Hansen for defendant—Applicant present

" Counsel moves motion for substitution of the defendant by

" B.A. Quarcoo current head of Onamroko-Adain family, counsel

" refers to supporting affidavit and other attachments ie.

" Exhibits A and B.

" Since the applicant is the current head of family he has been

" advised to apply to be substituted in the present action as

" defendant to prosecute the matter to its conclusion. We

" accordingly pray”.

" BY COURT:— Having heard Mr. Johnny Hansen of counsel for

" Defendant-Applicant and having read the accompanying affidavit

" and the other attachment I hereby grant the motion as prayed".

Comments:— My comments on the proceedings for August 22, 2002 is that when the learned trial Judge saw that the Appellant herein was absent in court, he should have complied with Order 52 Rule 6 supra and enquired from his court clerk whether the Appellant had been served or not before allowing the motion to be moved.

His failure to make the enquiry shows this disregard for established procedure/and it undermines the grant of the application he made in favour of Mr. B.A. Quarcoo.

The third and final process to be examined is the ORDER FOR SUBSTITUTION which is at page 14 of the Record of Appeal the operative part of this states as follows:—

" Upon reading the affidavit of Jafro Mensah Larkai of Accra

" The Deputy Head of Family of the Onamroko-Adain family elected

" on 25th March, 2002 the Application having been filed on

" the 16th day of July, 2002 in Support of Motion on Notice for an

" order for Substitution AND UPON HEARING JOHNNY HANSEN ESQ,

" Counsel for and on behalf of the Defendant-Applicant herein

" AND the Solicitor for Plaintiffs-Respondent having been served

" but absent.

" It is hereby ordered that B.A. Quarcoo, Esq., being elected

" as Head of Onamroko-Adain family be and is hereby substituted

" in place and stead of Mr. Paul Ayitey Tetteh the defendant in

" the above case, who is no longer Head of the Onamroko-Adain

" family”.

Comments:— The above formal order for substitution refers to the process filed on July 16, 2002 by Mr. B.A. Quarcoo as

"Motion on Notice" this is unfortunately not borne out by the record. It was at best on “Ex-Parte” application in disguise since it was not meant to be served on the appellant and was never served on him. From the foregoing it is very clear that the solicitor for Mr. B.A. Quarcoo breached Order 52 Rule 3 of L.N. 140A 1954 – The High Court Civil Procedure Rules.

The person who was to be substituted was not dead. He was alive and being alive the application for substitution made in the Court below should have been made on notice to him Mr. Paul Ayitey Tetteh. An examination of the 76-page Record of Appeal shows that nowhere in the record was a motion on notice filed. The learned trial judge, again erred by not finding out whether or not Mr. Paul Ayitey Tetteh had been served with the application for substitution before granting the prayer by B.A. Quarcoo, Esq., His omission to find out the reason for his absence offended against Order 52 Rule 6 of LN 140A of 1954. The High Court Civil Procedure Rules.

He erred in his duty and his order for substitution must be set aside and vacated and it is hereby set aside and vacated.

The Appeal accordingly succeeds with costs in favour of the Appellant Mr. Paul Ayitey Tetteh.

(SGD.)

K. OMARI-SASU

JUSTICE OF APPEAL COURT.

P.K. TWUMASI

I agree that the appeal should succeed.

(SGD.)
