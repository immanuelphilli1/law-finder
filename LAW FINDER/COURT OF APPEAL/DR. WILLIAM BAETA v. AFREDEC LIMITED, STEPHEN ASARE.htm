JUDGMENT

ESSILFIE-BONDZIE, J.A.:

This is an application for stay of the execution of the Ruling of the High Court delivered on the 15th November, 1999 in which the interpleader summons was dismissed summarily.

In considering application of this nature the court should bear in mind the balance of hardship. There is also the equally important principle that the applicant must demonstrate to the court that the appeal has a chance of success.

In this case, there is no dispute that on the 21st June, 1994 the plaintiff/judgment creditor obtained judgment against the defendant/judgment debtor herein for the sum of ¢10,720,031.33.  That on the 29th May, 1995 a consent judgment was also entered against the defendant/judgment debtor in the sum of $12,328.77 and £6,600 respectively with costs of  ¢2,500,000.00.

On the 9th May, 1997 the Registrar attached House Number J.856/5 Mile 11, Nungua, Accra belonging to Mrs. Clocanus Mensah, Managing Director of the defendant/judgment debtor’s company.

In a subsequent interpleader suit the claimant, the applicant herein, filed a statement of claim in which he averred that the property in dispute was sold to him by Mrs. Clocanus Mensah in 1977 with the consent and concurrence of the Nungua Traditional Council for an amount of  ¢195 million.  He also pleaded that the property in issue was at all material times the property of Mrs. Clocanus Mensah, the claimant’s vendor.

It is evident from the record before us that the claimant exhibited document marked as Exhibit 2(A) at the court below to support his claim.  In Exhibit 2(A) it was stated that Mrs. Clocanus Mensah received from the claimant two cheques drawn on the Social Security Bank for a total value of ¢195 million being the agreed price for subletting the property with the building for 97 years.

This being an interpleader action in respect of House Number J.856/5 Mile 11, Nungua, Accra, the law requires that since before the attachment the claimant/applicant was not in possession the burden is cast upon him to prove that at the time of the attachment he had already acquired title to the said property with the house on it. See LEMANU v. DUALA (YAKUBU) (Claimant) [1962] 1 G.L.R. 60.

Now a close look at Exhibit 2(A) shows that the said agreement exhibited by the Claimant/Applicant to prove title reveals that the two cheques received from the claimant/ applicant in payment of the agreed purchase price of ¢195 million were issued on the 13th August, 1997 and 15th August, 1997 respectively.  Now since it is not refuted that the Registrar attached House Number J.856/5 Mile 11, Nungua, Accra on the 9th May, 1977 it means that the claimant/applicant acquired the property from the Managing Director of the defendant/judgment debtor at the time when the property had already been attached. Besides Exhibit 2(A) also discloses that no specific property was mentioned in the said agreement nor was there any sipte plan attached to the agreement.  The said agreement is therefore not valid to confer title on the claimant/applicant.  I so hold.  See Conveyancing Decree [1973] (NRCD) 175 Sections 1(i) and 2.  The irresistible inference is that in order to prevent the execution of the property known as House Number J.856/5 Mile 11, Nungua, Mrs. Clocanus Mensah purported to sell it.

It is on record that in a further attempt to frustrate the execution of the judgment, the claimant/applicant has claimed that he subsequently bought the land on which the building stands from the Nungua Stool on the 10th February, 1998 long after the property had been attached. This obviously is an after thought and is not an evidence of ownership before the attachment of the property.

In the light of the foregoing, I share the view that the transactions between Mrs.  Clocanus Mensah and claimant/applicant and between the Nungua Stool and the applicant were fraudulent and calculated to defeat the whole execution process.  I hold that the applicant has failed to satisfy this court that his appeal has a likelihood of success.  The application for stay of execution is accordingly dismissed.

A. ESSILFIE-BONDZIE,

JUSTICE OF APPEAL.

TWUMASI, J.A.:

I  agree.
