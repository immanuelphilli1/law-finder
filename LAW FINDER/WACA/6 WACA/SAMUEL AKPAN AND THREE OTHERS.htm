                                                                         Lagos, 20th July, 1940.

COR. KINGDON, C.J CAREY AND BROOKE, JJ.

                                                                             REX

                                                                                 v         

                                                   . SAMUEL AKPAN AND THREE OTHERS

                                                     RE 1. JAMES WARIBO INYANG ETUK

                                                    2.   JOHN UDO IBANGA

                                                    3.   SAMPSON OK PONG

 

                                                  

Appeal Court, 20th July, 1940.

 

    The fact that an accomplice Pleading guilty was not sentenced giving evidence for the Crown will not cause a conviction quashed provided that the Judge warned himself adequate, to the necessity for corroboration and that there was in fact 511 corroboration.

Rex v. Robert Smith (18 Criminal Appeal Reports p.19) followed. Held: Conviction upheld.

There is no need to set out the facts.

J. S. R. Cole for Crown.

The following joint judgment was delivered :­

KINGDON, C.J., CAREY AND BROOKE, JJ.

In this case the three appellants were charged with possession of apparatus for making counterfeit coins contra section 148 (3) (c) of the Criminal Code. They were tried not jointly with each other but also jointly with a fourth man nil' Samuel Akpan who was charged with the same offence but slightly different particulars.

The charges arose out of the same transaction. S Akpan pleaded guilty and was then called as the first and principal witness for the prosecution. The learned Trial Judge found the accused guilty and sentenced them accordingly. The principal question that arises upon the appeal concerns the calling of the  man Samuel Akpan as a witness for the prosecution before he been sentenced. This question was considered by the Court of Criminal Appeal in England in the case of R. v. Robert S (18 Crim. App. Rep. p.19) when Avory, J. in giving the judgment of the Court said :-

"Without saying that in every case it is proper to sentence an accomplice pleading guilty before he gives evidence for the Crown. it is obvious that where he is not sentenced it is more than ever necessary to warn the jury about accepting his testimony, because, naturally, his object is to mitigate his own punishment."

In this case, we think that, without question, the proper course was for sentence to have been passed upon Samuel Akpan before he was allowed to be called for the Crown, But the fact that this was not done will not lead us to quash the convictions provided that the Judge warned himself adequately as to the necessity for corroboration, and that there was in fact sufficient corroboration. As to this the' Judge did warn himself very fully as to the necessity for corroboration and gave very special attention to the question of corroboration, As to the sufficiency of the corroboration there was in the case of the 3rd appellant a~ple, and in the case of the 1st and 2nd appellants, though not very much, sufficient in law.

The appeals of all the appellants are accordingly dismissed.
